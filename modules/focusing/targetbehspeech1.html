Instructions

Read the following client speech and identify potential target behavior changes that you could choose to focus on in the session. Remember target behaviors are actions a patient can take to change, they are often behaviors that can be increased or decreased.

Feedback

Correct answers:

  • Partying less
  • Visiting the psychiatrist
  • Taking medications correctly

Client: Yeah, so I’ve had a really rough week. Tons of stuff going on. Yesterday I got fired from my job for showing up late. I was partying the night before and slept through my alarm. I was planning to quit anyway since I am leaving for the summer but it doesn’t look good. And then my dad got really mad at me when I let him know about it. But that’s nothing new. So I’ve been feeling really down. I should probably visit my psychiatrist again because I don’t think these meds are working the way they should. I just sometimes feel like I can’t get up in the morning and that it’s maybe because of them too. I don’t remember to take them all the time either which I know isn’t good. I sometimes think that I should just quit taking everything and like move into a tiny house and go on a wilderness retreat. I have a friend that did that…
