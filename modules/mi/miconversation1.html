Feedback

1. What is the counselor’s style?
Interested in quantity and frequency assessments of drinking, persuasive or overly directive of patient, almost feels as if the counselor is not listening to the client- as if they are in different conversations
2. Feelings after that conversation: You? The client?
Everyone has a different reaction to this conversation. For some it may be similar to how they currently approach drinking problems and for others this may seem so disconnected that it is almost offensive or confusing. The client is open to responding to the questions but seems to be reassuring the counselor repeatedly they are not an alcoholic- it doesn’t seem as if there is a connection here between counselor and client.
3. What progress is the client making?
The client ended by saying “ I’m not an alcoholic you know” meaning they were likely moving towards being defensive and not making a change

Client: I panic, I just feel so full of shakes that I take a drink before I leave the house

Counselor: Yes it will be good to drink less. How much do drink these days

Client: Well, I’m not an alcoholic its just that I need it before I leave the house

Counselor: How much would you say you drink each week, even if it’s just a guess?

Client: Well you know, I don’t know, I only drink beer so maybe just a few bottles a day, sometimes more; I need it to calm down really

Counselor: Yes, I see that’s probably at least around 20-30 bottles a week. Do you drink even when you go to pick up the kids

Client: I don’t like to drink as much as I do, but when I am nervous, then I take a drink but it’s really not very much

Counselor: yes, and how often does this happen when you are with the children?

Client: Well I don’t always drink before I go out to get the kids, but you have no idea how terrified I get, it’s like I’m in a daze. Today I had to hang on to a lamppost to keep steady, and not faint. It’s just horrible.

Counselor: and did you have a drink before you came out?

Client: Just a little one to be honest but I’m not an alcoholic you know
