Instructions

Complete this worksheet for a target behavior that you or a client has struggled to change. When we think about making changes, most of us don’t really consider all “sides” of the issue in a complete way. Instead, we often do what we think we “should” do, avoid doing things we don’t feel like doing, or just feel confused or overwhelmed and give up thinking about it at all. Thinking through the pros and cons of both making and not making a change is one way to help us fully consider a possible change. This can help us to “hang on” to our plan in times of stress or temptation. For most people, “making a change” will probably mean quit doing something or start doing something else, but it is important that you consider what specific change you might want to make. Write in the reasons that you can think of in each of the boxes below. You may choose to start in the upper left box and work your way clockwise.
