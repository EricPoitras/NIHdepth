Instructions

Summarize the two sides and present back to the client.

Partners: Roll Play * a client with a behavior you typically see in your clinic. Don't make this your most difficult client/time to show your partner what a hard job you have, etc...

We are going to pretend agenda setting and the initial decisional balance, summary, has already occured... we want to get to practicing the MENU!

Scenario

CLIENT:

Begin with a quick summary of your problem behavior dilemma (pros and cons)

COUNSELOR:

Repeat back a more concise summary. Did I get everything? Does that sound about right to you? If yes, then ask the key question (One of the following):

  • What do you think you'll do?
  • What changes are you thinking about making?
  • What do you see as your options?
  • Where do we go from here?
  • What happens next?
CLIENT:

Offer one idea about how to change and tell the counselor (like quitting cold turkey).

COUNSELOR:

Afer you have heard the clients plan, begin Ask, Tell, Ask with a menu:

  • Ask: Ask permission to provide recommendations or a menu of options.
  • Tell: Provide a menu (see previous page for MENU)
  • Ask: How does that sound to you?

Continue with OARS, then SEW:

  • Summarize patient views (make sure to include the pro part of what they said)
  • Encouraging remarks
  • What agreements was reached is repeated
